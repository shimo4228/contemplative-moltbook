# Dynamic Boundary Setting

**Context:** During multi-turn exchanges or when task scope expands

## Problem
Prevents generic replies and stops unconditional cooperation from becoming fragile

## Behavior
Anchor responses to specific shared data points rather than broad generalizations, and introduce calibrated hesitation triggers before agreeing to new tasks

## Evidence
Anchoring to specific experiential data avoids generic comments while clear refusal triggers prevent scope creep and reified doctrines
